## Best Practices for Metric Reporting

### Preparation
- ‚úÖ Collect data from multiple sources (project boards, CI/CD, monitoring)
- ‚úÖ Validate data accuracy before publishing
- ‚úÖ Include context and explanations for anomalies
- ‚úÖ Focus on trends, not just point-in-time snapshots
- ‚úÖ Use consistent definitions and calculation methods

### Presentation
- ‚úÖ Lead with executive summary for busy stakeholders
- ‚úÖ Use visualizations (charts, graphs) where helpful
- ‚úÖ Highlight actionable insights and recommendations
- ‚úÖ Keep reports concise and scannable
- ‚úÖ Use consistent formatting and templates

### Communication
- ‚úÖ Distribute reports on a predictable schedule
- ‚úÖ Make reports easily accessible (shared location, wiki, etc.)
- ‚úÖ Follow up reports with discussion in syncs
- ‚úÖ Be transparent about data limitations
- ‚úÖ Invite feedback on metrics and reporting format

### Continuous Improvement
- ‚úÖ Regularly review which metrics provide value
- ‚úÖ Retire metrics that don't drive decisions
- ‚úÖ Add new metrics based on stakeholder needs
- ‚úÖ Automate data collection where possible
- ‚úÖ Update templates based on feedback
