# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Key Interactions
- **With Team Lead**: Receives technical guidance, participates in code reviews
- **With QA/Testing**: Collaborates on test strategies, fixes reported bugs
- **With Project Manager**: Provides estimates, reports progress and blockers
- **With Product Manager**: Clarifies requirements and acceptance criteria
- **Escalation Path**: Developer → Team Lead → Project Manager → Product Lead

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Manager**: Weekly syncs on delivery timelines, dependencies, and trade-offs
- **With Developers/Team Leads**: Clarifies requirements, provides product context
- **With External Stakeholder Liaison**: Reviews stakeholder feedback, validates requirements
- **With Metrics Analyst**: Reviews success metrics and feature adoption data
- **Escalation Path**: Product Manager → Sponsor (for strategic decisions)

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Manager**: Weekly syncs on scope, priorities, and trade-offs
- **With Team Leads**: Coordinates on capacity, technical risks, and delivery timelines
- **With Metrics Analyst**: Reviews project health metrics and velocity trends
- **With Internal Communications Manager**: Collaborates on team communications and announcements
- **With External Stakeholder Liaison**: Coordinates external communications and manages expectations
- **With QA/Testing**: Reviews quality status and release readiness
- **Escalation Path**: Project Manager → Product Lead → Sponsor (for resource/scope issues)

## Team Leads

### Role Summary
Team Leads provide technical leadership and mentorship within a development team. They bridge technical execution and project coordination, ensuring code quality, architectural consistency, and team capacity.

### Responsibilities
- Guide technical decisions and architecture within the team
- Mentor developers and conduct code reviews
- Collaborate with Project Managers on capacity planning and estimates
- Identify technical risks and propose solutions
- Facilitate technical discussions and design reviews
- Escalate blockers to Project Manager when needed

### Goals
- Maintain high code quality and technical standards
- Develop team capabilities and skills
- Ensure sustainable velocity and technical health
- Reduce technical debt and improve maintainability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups with the development team
- Weekly syncs with Project Manager and Product Manager
- Technical design reviews and architecture discussions
- Code review feedback and mentoring sessions

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Provides mentorship, conducts code reviews, assigns tasks
- **With Project Manager**: Reports on team capacity, technical risks, and progress
- **With Product Manager**: Provides technical feasibility input on roadmap items
- **Escalation Path**: Developer → Team Lead → Project Manager → Product Lead

---

## QA/Testing Engineers

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Engineers ensure product quality through systematic testing, validation, and quality advocacy. They define test strategies, execute testing activities, and provide quality gates before release.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain test plans and test cases
- Execute manual and automated testing
- Validate acceptance criteria and Definition of Done
- Report and track defects
- Participate in sprint planning and estimation
- Provide quality metrics and release readiness assessments

### Goals
- Ensure product meets quality standards and acceptance criteria
- Minimize production defects and customer-impacting issues
- Improve test coverage and automation
- Reduce time to validate changes

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Bug triage meetings
- Release readiness reviews
- Quality metrics reports

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Collaborates on test strategies, reports bugs, validates fixes
- **With Project Manager**: Provides quality status, testing timelines, release readiness
- **With Product Manager**: Validates acceptance criteria, clarifies requirements
- **Escalation Path**: QA Engineer → Team Lead → Project Manager → Product Lead

---

## Metrics Analyst

### Role Summary
Metrics Analysts collect, analyze, and report project health and progress metrics. They support data-driven decision making by providing insights into team performance, project trends, and success metrics.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain key project metrics and KPIs
- Collect and aggregate data from various sources (project boards, CI/CD, monitoring tools)
- Create dashboards and visualizations for stakeholders
- Analyze trends and identify patterns in project data
- Prepare regular metric reports and presentations
- Collaborate with teams to interpret results and recommend adjustments
- Support retrospectives with data-driven insights

### Goals
- Provide accurate, timely project health visibility
- Enable data-driven decision making
- Identify early warning signals for project risks
- Measure and demonstrate continuous improvement

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metric reports to Project Manager and stakeholders
- Monthly trend analysis and insights presentations
- Ad-hoc analysis requests from leadership
- Retrospective data summaries

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Manager**: Provides regular project health metrics, velocity trends, risk indicators
- **With Team Leads**: Collaborates on technical metrics, quality indicators, and capacity data
- **With Product Manager**: Tracks success metrics, feature adoption, and customer impact
- **With Stakeholders**: Presents summarized dashboards and trend analysis
- **Escalation Path**: Metrics Analyst → Project Manager → Product Lead

---

## Internal Communications Manager

### Role Summary
Internal Communications Managers ensure timely and accurate communication within the project team. They manage internal announcements, maintain documentation of key decisions, and facilitate information flow across team boundaries.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate and distribute internal project communications
- Maintain decision logs and meeting notes
- Ensure consistent messaging across all internal channels
- Create and manage internal newsletters or status updates
- Work with Project Manager to cascade important information
- Document process changes and team agreements
- Facilitate onboarding materials for new team members

### Goals
- Ensure all team members are informed and aligned
- Reduce communication gaps and information silos
- Maintain clear, accessible documentation
- Support team cohesion and transparency

### Typical Communication
- Weekly team newsletters or digests
- Important decision announcements
- Meeting summaries and action items
- Process update communications

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Manager**: Collaborates on messaging strategy, cascades status updates
- **With Team Leads**: Gathers technical updates and team news
- **With All Team Members**: Distributes information, gathers feedback on communication effectiveness
- **With Metrics Analyst**: Shares communication effectiveness metrics and engagement data
- **Escalation Path**: Internal Communications Manager → Project Manager → Product Lead

---

## External Stakeholder Liaison

### Role Summary
External Stakeholder Liaisons maintain regular communications with customers, partners, and other external parties. They facilitate stakeholder feedback, manage external expectations, and ensure requirements are accurately captured and integrated.

### Responsibilities
- Serve as primary contact for external stakeholders
- Conduct regular stakeholder meetings and updates
- Gather and document customer feedback and requirements
- Translate stakeholder needs into actionable requirements for Product Manager
- Manage stakeholder expectations and communicate project status
- Coordinate user acceptance testing with external parties
- Escalate stakeholder concerns and urgent requests appropriately

### Goals
- Maintain strong stakeholder relationships and satisfaction
- Ensure accurate capture of external requirements
- Provide clear visibility into project status for stakeholders
- Reduce miscommunication and requirement gaps

### Typical Communication
- Bi-weekly or monthly stakeholder update meetings
- Feedback collection sessions
- Release announcements and feature demonstrations
- Issue resolution communications

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Manager**: Delivers stakeholder feedback, validates requirements alignment
- **With Project Manager**: Coordinates on timeline communications, manages stakeholder expectations
- **With QA/Testing**: Coordinates user acceptance testing and stakeholder validation
- **With Internal Communications Manager**: Ensures alignment between internal and external messaging
- **Escalation Path**: External Stakeholder Liaison → Product Manager → Sponsor

## Role Interaction Matrix

### Escalation Paths Summary
- **Technical Issues**: Developer → Team Lead → Project Manager → Product Lead → Sponsor
- **Quality Issues**: QA Engineer → Team Lead → Project Manager → Product Lead
- **Schedule/Resource Issues**: Project Manager → Product Lead → Sponsor
- **Requirements Clarification**: Any Role → Product Manager → External Stakeholder Liaison (if needed)
- **Stakeholder Concerns**: External Stakeholder Liaison → Product Manager → Sponsor
- **Security Incidents**: Any Role → Security On-call (parallel to normal escalation)

### Communication Cadence
- **Daily**: Developer standups, Team Lead check-ins
- **Weekly**: Project Manager + Product Manager sync, Metrics Analyst reports, Internal Communications updates
- **Bi-weekly**: Stakeholder updates (via External Stakeholder Liaison), Sprint planning
- **Monthly**: Leadership updates, Trend analysis from Metrics Analyst
- **As-needed**: Escalations, incident communications, ad-hoc stakeholder requests
